## Tellingly Launches a Daily Question App That Shows Users How Rare Their Instincts Are

CHICAGO, IL - June 2, 2026 - Tellingly today launched its web beta, a daily identity app built around one sharp question, live answer distributions, rarity scores, and friend compatibility links.

Each day, Tellingly asks users a playful but revealing question: a moral dilemma, secret habit, hypothetical, or preference. After answering in one tap, users immediately see how everyone else answered, how rare their choice was, and a short read on what the answer suggests about their operating pattern.

"The unit of value is one sentence someone screenshots because it tells them something true about themselves they could not have said first," said the founder of Tellingly.

Unlike static quizzes, Tellingly becomes more valuable as more people answer. Every answer improves the live distribution and rarity score for the next user. The app's growth loop is a compatibility link: users send the same question set to a friend and both see where their instincts match and where they diverge, without building a public profile or social graph.

Tellingly is available now in web beta at https://tellingly.zandrews77.workers.dev. An iOS version is planned for App Store release.

## About Tellingly

Tellingly is a daily identity ritual built around sharp questions, live rarity scores, private pattern maps, and friend compatibility links. It is designed to feel playful and intimate without requiring public profiles, feeds, or social graphs.
