# Tellingly Launch Plan

Prepared on June 2, 2026.

## Objective

Launch Tellingly as a daily identity ritual and drive the first population of answer data.

Primary 30-day goals:

- 10,000 web app visits.
- 2,500 completed daily sets.
- 1,000 comparison links created.
- 500 iOS waitlist emails.
- 150 screenshot/card shares tagged or submitted.

## Audience

Primary: curious, socially fluent people ages 18-34 who already share personality, dating, friendship, and "what would you do?" content in group chats.

Secondary: creators and newsletter writers who like prompts, identity language, and conversation starters.

## Core Message

Tellingly tells you how rare your instincts are, one sharp question at a time.

## Positioning

Not astrology. Not clinical psychometrics. Not a chatbot.

Tellingly is a daily ritual with a live population behind it: every answer makes the next person's rarity score more accurate.

## Launch Phases

### Phase 1: Soft Seed, Days -14 to -7

Goal: collect early data and find the first sticky verdict lines.

Actions:

- Share private beta link with 50-100 trusted users.
- Ask each tester to send one comparison link to a friend.
- Screenshot and catalog the best weekly-card verdicts.
- Track completion rate, comparison-link creation rate, and which questions drive the most shares.

### Phase 2: Curiosity Tease, Days -7 to -1

Goal: make the app feel like something people want to test about themselves.

Actions:

- Post daily dilemma questions without explaining the full app.
- Publish 3-5 short Reels/TikToks built around "What percent of people would do this?"
- Start the waitlist CTA: "Get the iOS drop."
- Invite micro-creators to answer a question on camera.

### Phase 3: Public Launch, Days 0 to 7

Goal: concentrate traffic so the live distribution feels alive.

Actions:

- Launch the web app link.
- Post the hero Reel and pin it.
- Publish the launch thread/post across X, Threads, LinkedIn, TikTok, and Instagram.
- Send launch email.
- Ask early users to post their weekly card with the line visible.

### Phase 4: Ritual Loop, Days 8 to 30

Goal: turn novelty into habit.

Actions:

- Release one new public question prompt per day.
- Weekly "rarest answers this week" recap.
- Creator duet/stitch prompts: "Would you have answered like me?"
- Retarget waitlist and web visitors with iOS launch promise.

## Channel Strategy

| Channel | Role | Cadence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| TikTok/Reels | Awareness and proof of vibe | 4-5 per week |
| Instagram Stories | Daily question habit | Daily |
| Threads/X | Fast prompt distribution | 1-2 per day |
| Email | Launch and retention | Weekly |
| Product Hunt | Web launch spike | Launch week |
| Creator outreach | Social proof | 20 micro-creators/week |

## Metrics

- Visit-to-answer-start rate.
- Completion rate for five-question set.
- Answer-to-share-card download rate.
- Answer-to-comparison-link creation rate.
- Comparison-link open-to-completion rate.
- Waitlist email conversion.
- D7 return rate.

## Creative Rules

- Lead with the question, not the feature.
- Use exact percentages and verdict lines as proof.
- Make every post answerable in the comments.
- Keep copy intimate but safe: no diagnoses, no moral ranking, no "you are broken" language.
- Position rarity as difference, not superiority.
